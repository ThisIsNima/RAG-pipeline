TITLE: Enzyme Promiscuity: A Mechanistic and Evolutionary Perspective
YEAR: 2010
TOPIC: history
OPENALEX_ID: https://openalex.org/W2128312954

Many, if not most, enzymes can promiscuously catalyze reactions, or act on substrates, other than those for which they evolved. Here, we discuss the structural, mechanistic, and evolutionary implications of this manifestation of infidelity of molecular recognition. We define promiscuity and related phenomena and also address their generality and physiological implications. Finally, we address the hypothesis that promiscuous enzymatic activities serve as evolutionary starting points and highlight the unique evolutionary features of promiscuous enzyme functions.
